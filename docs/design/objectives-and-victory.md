# Objectives and Victory Design Doctrine

## Purpose

Scenarios should be driven by objectives, not only annihilation.

Empire battles and skirmishes often involve holding, delaying, reaching, protecting, disrupting, or extracting.

## Objective Types

Early objective types may include:

- hold area
- contest area
- reach location
- delay enemy
- break through
- protect unit
- withdraw in order
- survive for duration

## Objective Behaviour

Objectives should influence captain orders and unit posture.

A unit ordered to hold should not abandon its objective just because an enemy is nearby.

A unit ordered to delay may fall back fighting rather than seek decisive engagement.

A unit ordered to break through may accept cohesion loss to push through.

## Victory

Victory should be scenario-specific.

Possible measures:

- objective held at final tick
- time delayed
- number of units routed
- casualty ratio
- cohesion remaining
- successful withdrawal
- enemy advance prevented
- key unit survival

## Design Rule

The simulation should report why a side won or failed, not only who remained alive.
