# Roles and Loadouts Design Doctrine

## Purpose

Roles should affect behaviour, preferred distance, confidence, pressure, and group function.

Roles should not simply be stat bundles.

## Role Principles

A role defines:

- preferred engagement distance
- preferred position in formation
- confidence triggers
- pressure weaknesses
- movement tendencies
- target preferences
- group utility

## Example Roles

### Shield Fighter

Preferred behaviour:

- hold front line
- protect nearby allies
- close distance when confidence and order allow
- resist pressure from frontal attacks

Weaknesses:

- hesitation when closing into reach weapons
- vulnerable if isolated or flanked
- may block allies if poorly positioned

### Spear Fighter

Preferred behaviour:

- maintain reach
- punish overextension
- fight from behind or beside allies
- avoid being rushed

Weaknesses:

- pressured by close enemies
- poor if crowded
- vulnerable if line collapses

### Polearm Fighter

Preferred behaviour:

- threaten at reach
- exploit openings
- support front line
- punish committed enemies

Weaknesses:

- needs space
- vulnerable when rushed
- can be disrupted by friendly crowding

### Recruit

Preferred behaviour:

- follow group shape
- avoid stepping forward first
- hold when supported
- degrade when isolated

Weaknesses:

- hesitation
- panic
- poor recovery under pressure
- unreliable aggression

### Veteran

Preferred behaviour:

- maintain formation
- recover from pressure faster
- fill gaps
- act under imperfect conditions

Weaknesses:

- still vulnerable to being isolated, overwhelmed, or exhausted

## Design Rule

A role should produce recognisable behaviour before it produces numeric advantage.
